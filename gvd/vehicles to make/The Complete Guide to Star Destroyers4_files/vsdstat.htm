Victory I- and II-class Star Destroyers

The Clone Wars brought about many technological advances in the Old Republic. The greatest of these achievements was the Victory Star Destroyer. The Victory-class Star Destroyer was designed by Republic engineer Walex Blissex near the end of the Clone Wars. It was the most powerful ship of its time, dwarfing the Republic Dreadnaught by 300 meters. However, the Victory Star Destroyer saw little action during the Republic. Later, after Palpatine had declared himself Emperor and incorporated the Imperial-class Star Destroyer into the fleet, the ships were only used for guarding unimportant systems and bases. But when the Rebellion was at its height, the Victory ships got to do there part as well. By the time Grand Admiral Thrawn took command of the Empire, the Victory was being used almost as much as the Imperials. Five years later, the warlord High Admiral Teradoc had an entire fleet consisting of nothing but Victory Star Destroyers, and they were just as powerful as his rival's fleet of Imperial Star Destroyers. The Victory Star Destroyer is still very much a part of the present-day Imperial fleet.

The Victory Star Destroyer is excellent for planetary assault and defense, as they are the only Star Destroyer that can enter a planet's gravity. The huge atmospheric maneuvering fins on either side of the ship along with its massive repulsorlift generators enable it to do that. This does, however, limit the Victory's ship-to-ship combat. Also, the repulsors give the Victory a very slow sublight speed, but that is made up for by the amazingly fast hyperdrive. The Victory also has two squadrons of TIE fighters, as well as planetary assault troops.

The Victory II-class Star Destroyer solves most of these problems. The Victory II was made just after the Clone Wars. The Victory II does not have the repulsors or the fins like the Victory I, which gives the Victory II a greater sublight speed than the much slower Victory I. Also, the Victory II is better for ship-to-ship combat, but the missile launchers had to be sacrificed. The Victory II has better weapons, a stronger hull, and, consequently, a larger crew. The Victory IIs are especially good when working in teams, and they can easily stop most any opponent. Very few Victory IIs were made, though. They were produced at the end of the Clone Wars, and when the wars ended, the Old Republic stopped making Victory IIs.
