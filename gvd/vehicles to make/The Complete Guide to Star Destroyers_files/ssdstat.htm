Super Star Destroyers

It is interesting that one of the greatest warships ever built was never given its due respect by the Rebel Alliance. Many histories persist in calling Lord Vader's flagship a "super star destroyer." This is a very loose and overused term the Rebels have given a number of ships, regardless of their actual class. Any Star Destroyer larger than the standard Imperator-class is called a Super Star Destroyer, whether it is the 2 km long Allegiance or the 17 km long Eclipse. In the interest of continuity, the Executor and similar ships have been classified as Super Star Destroyers. This entry will only include statistics for the most common of the "Super-class" vessels: the Executor-class command ship. The Eclipse- and Sovereign-classes have their own entry, while "oddities," such as the Allegiance and Shockwave, are only briefly described here. For more information on the other Super Star Destroyers, read their individual entries in the Complete List of Star Destroyer Names.

Executor-class Star Destroyer

After the destruction of the first Death Star, Darth Vader ordered the construction of the first of a new class of vessels. Designed by Lira Wessex, designer of the Imperator-class Star Destroyer, the Executor-class Star Destroyer was born. The first of this line, the Executor, was an amazing feat. At 8 kilometers long, the Executor was five times the size of an Imperial Star Destroyer. The Executor was also five times as powerful, with 1,000 weapon emplancements. The huge vessel had 12 squadrons (two wings) of TIE fighters, plus many ground assault troops. Three other Executor-class ships were made before the Battle of Hoth, though many more were made between then and Endor. Amazingly, by the time Grand Admiral Thrawn took command of the Empire, there were no longer any Super Star Destroyers in Imperial hands. More were made after Thrawn's death, each more advanced than the originals. But the Executor-class Star Destroyer remained the ultimate ship in the galaxy.

The Super Star Destroyer was an 8 km long behemoth. It could take on whole fleets by itself. Nothing the Rebels had could compete with the mighty craft. Though supposedly the Rebels did capture one Super from the Empire, they scrapped it since they believed that many smaller ships were better than one giant vessel. The Super Star Destroyer does have the downside of being slow and combersome. Despite the vast amount of weapontry aboard the ship, it often served the role of command ship, guiding huge fleets of vessels.

The Super Star Destroyer design has been slightly altered through the years. The Terror had a cloaking device. The Night Hammer had black stealth armor, as well as several additional squadrons of TIE fighters. In fact, all late-production Supers had a third shield generator tower along their centerline. Also, the Sovereign- and Eclipse-class Super Star Destroyers (see seperate entry) were greatly improved from the original Super-class design.


AUTHOR'S NOTE ON THE EXECUTOR'S LENGTH: I have received many e-mails regarding a website known as the Star Wars Technical Commentaries and its discussion of the true length of the Executor. I read this web page long before I created this website, and have confirmed a great deal of the author's data firsthand. I agree that ILM designed the Executor to be eleven times the size of an Imperial Star Destroyer, not the five-times-as-long stated by West End Game's Imperial Sourcebook. However, in the interest of continuity, I have kept the WEG length of 8,000 meters. All of the novels, sourcebooks and comics in the Expanded Universe of Star Wars have used WEG's stats, and I feel that changing them here would cause too much confusion. The number of crew, weaponry, and carried starfighters is based on the length of 8 km, and we have no official stats for the 11-mile-long Executor. Also, the Eclipse was meant to be twice the length of the Executor, which at 10 miles long, it obviously would not be. So, in conclusion, while I personally feel the Executor was meant to be 11 miles long, the "official" length has now become 5 miles, and I am sticking to that.

Allegiance-class Star Destroyer

One noteworthy class of "Super Star Destroyer" is the Allegiance-class. At least four of this class have been positively identified, with several more unconfirmed sightings. At 2.2 km long, the Allegiance is significantly longer than an Imperator-class Star Destroyer, thus provoking the Rebels to deem it a "Super Star Destroyer." The ship resembles an enlarged Imperator-class, but lacks the main hanger bays and other minor hull features. Most likely this ship was designed to coordinate large-scale attacks; exactly the function the Allegiance was serving at the Battle of Calamari. Three other Allegiance-class ships were sighted escorting the Emperor's flagship Eclipse to the Rebel base at Pinnacle Moon. The names of these vessels was not confirmed, and they were either scattered or destroyed by the Emperor's rampaging Force storm. No other Allegiance-class command ships have been positively identified, but it is possible that the Star Destroyer Shockwave was a derivative of this class.

Other "Super Star Destroyers"

Over the course of the Empire's 25-year building spree, many ships classified as Star Destroyers were longer than the standard 1.6 km Imperator-class Star Destroyer. Since many of these vessels were unique and, for the most part, unidentified, this guide will not cover them in great detail. Many of these are like the Shockwave (Warlord Harsk's flagship), built as command ships for fleets that did not rate an Executor-class vessel. These "super star destroyers" vary in size and capacity, ranging from 2 km to over 10 km. Because a great number of these ships were kept in reserve in the Deep Core, and many were lost with the destruction of the Imperial throneworld of Byss, they will never be fully cataloged.
