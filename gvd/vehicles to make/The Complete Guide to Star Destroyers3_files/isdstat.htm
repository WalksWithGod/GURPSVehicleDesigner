Imperial Star Destroyers

The Imperial Star Destroyer is truly a marvel of modern engineering. While earlier vessels had been longer, few of them were as powerful or as massive as the Imperator. While the designation "star destroyer" is a misnomer, since the ISD is classified as a star battlecruiser, the Imperator was seen as the direct successor to the Victory. It may also have been kind of a threat: if the Empire's destroyers were a mile long, how big would an Imperial battleship be?

Over the years, there have been many modifications and variations on the Imperator design. Minor hull features and the placement of sensors have created numerous models of the ISD, but all are based on two main classes: the Imperator I and the Imperator II. In addition to these, other Star Destroyers have been modified to serve as command ships, communication vessels, interdictor cruisers, heavy bombardment warships, and countless other variations. Several of these are discussed below; the most obvious type, the heavily modified New Republic Star Destroyer, has its own entry.

Imperator I-class Star Destroyer

The dawn of a new era under Emperor Palpatine saw the creation of a new class of Star Destroyer. The mighty Imperator was designed by Lira Wessex, the daughter of the Victory Star Destroyer's designer.

The Senate fought the design, pointing to the enormous cost of construction and the amount of fund that would be needed to maintain even one of these warships. But the newly strengthened Imperial Navy demanded that the ship be built, and the Emperor agreed with them. Thus Kuat Drive Yards produced the first of the 1.6 km long Imperator-class Star Destroyer (and only 50 million credits over budget, too).

Grand Moff Tarkin, creator of the Death Star battlestation, once said: "It has long been my contention that the average citizen has no grasp of numbers nor a head for calculation. I maintain that one of the reasons for the effectiveness of the Star Destroyer is its size. When citizens look at a Star Destroyer and then look at the craft which might be made available to attack it, event he best mind among them wishes to reject the notion rather than approach the problem tactically." While Tarkin is undoubtedly underestimating the destructive firepower of the Imperator-class Star Destroyer, his point is certainly valid. A ship so large that it can swallow up the craft that make up most planetary defense navies, not to mention deploy hundreds of its own carried craft, has its advantages. The Imperial Star Destroyer graphically demonstrates Emperor Palpatine's philosophy of "rule through fear of force".

The Imperial Navy poured billions of credits into the Star Destroyer project. This mighty vessel, which could turn an entire civilization into ruin, soon became the backbone of the Imperial Starfleet. Over 120 heavy weaponry can decimate enemy fleets, while the full wing of 72 TIE fighters can overwhelm enemy starfighters. Virtually a fortress in space, the ISD also carries ground assault troops, including an entire division of Stormtroopers.

Between the time of the launching of the original Imperator and the rebirth of Emperor Palpatine, over 20,000 Star Destroyers were built. No ship built during this period could match the Star Destroyer in open combat, though two of the Rebel Mon Calamarian monstrosities could perhaps defeat it. While the MC90 Star Cruiser and later Mon Cal designs are superior craft, the Star Destroyer is still not outclassed 25 years after the Battle of Endor. In an attempt to improve the lifespan of existing ships, the Empire has upgraded weapons systems and installed cloaking devices on most of their ships. During the Imperator-class Star Destroyer's 50-year service it has seen as many failures as it has successes. Nevertheless, the ship remains an excellent design and a testimony to Imperial might.

Imperator II-class Star Destroyer

Approximately one year after the Battle of Yavin, a new subclass of the Imperial Star Destroyer began appearing. It is not known exactly when the first Imperator II Star Destroyers entered service, because their construction was kept top secret. Needless to say, the Rebel Alliance fleet was stunned when they first encountered a Mark II.

The original Imperial Star Destroyer was considered a masterpiece, but it was not without flaws. The Imperial Navy decided that heavier firepower was needed for planetary bombardment, and that 60 ion cannons were far more than were necessary. Therefore, the Imperator II design removed forty ion cannons and replaced them with heavy turbolasers. The hyperdrive was improved, giving the Mark II an impressive x1 hyperdrive. The particle shielding was also increased, probably in response to the destruction of the Death Star and the threat of Rebel torpedo attacks.

The Mark II's were rarities for a long time, but eventually all new Star Destroyers were of this class. Once Star Destroyer construction was restarted under Grand Admiral Thrawn, it is believed that only Mark II's were built by the remaining Imperial shipyards. The Imperator II is able to hold its own against modern New Republic warships such as the MC90, and is still used by the Imperial Navy over 20 years after the Battle of Endor.

Special Cases

Over the fifty year period during which the Empire constructed Imperator-class Star Destroyers of one kind or another, many modified ships were developed. Some were merely test beds for new propulsion and weapons systems, but most were modified for a specific purpose. Some have become their own classes, including the Interdictor Star Destroyer described below, and the Allegiance-class Command Ship, described elsewhere in this guide.

Communications Ships

The only know Star Destroyer designed as a communications ship was at the battle of Endor. This slightly larger Star Destroyer (whose name is unknown) was responsible for the broad-band jamming that prevented the Rebels from detecting the Imperial fleet and the status of the Death Star's deflector shield. It was as well protected as a regular Star Destroyer, but may have had fewer guns. Several standard Star Destroyers worked as transmission relays for this main communications ship, including the Imperator II-class Star Destroyer Avenger. The communications ship was the target of a major starfighter Rebel attack, and was destroyed. No other dedicated communications ships are known.

Command Ships

Before the Executor-class Super Star Destroyer was available in large numbers, most fleets were led by slightly modified Imperator-class Star Destroyers. Often the modifications would only include larger officer quarters and additional communications equipment. Some command ships would be more heavily armored, or even include advanced tactical computers for fleet coordination. The more significant flagships were usually larger as well, such as the post-Endor flagship of Warlord Harrsk, the Shockwave. The ISD Majestic, which served as the Emperor's flagship, was modified to include chambers for the Emperor and his guardsmen. In fact, any ship that might serve as the Emperor's flagship (including all Executor-class vessels) also included a throne room, much like the one on the second Death Star.

TIE Compliment aboard Imperial Star Destroyers

The most common TIE compliment, listed in the statistics above, was standardized shortly before the Battle of Endor. The earliest Star Destroyers carried a motley assortment of fighters, mainly consisting of the original TIE Starfighter. Several years before the destruction of the first Death Star, one fighter squadron was replaced with a squadron of TIE/gt Ground Support fighter/bombers. With the invention of the TIE Bomber, all TIE/gt were replaced by the time of the Battle of Hoth. When the TIE Interceptor entered service, they began slowly replacing TIE fighter squadrons. At first, most Star Destroyers only carried one squadron of Interceptors, but by the time of the Battle of Endor, most ships carried the standard compliment mentioned above. After the Battle of Endor, the Empire concentrated on producing new versions of the TIE Interceptor (which included shields), and slowly phased out the original TIE Starfighter. While some Destroyers carried specialty craft, such as TIE Defenders, TIE/D droid fighters, and Scimitar Assault Bombers, most Star Destroyers carried shielded TIE Interceptors and one squadron of Bombers until 15 to 20 years after the Battle of Endor. Around the time of the peace treaty between the Empire and New Republic, many Imperial Star Destroyers also carried SoroSuub Preybird Assault Fighters, which were obtained from questionable third-party sources, and not the pro-Alliance SoroSuub Corporation.
