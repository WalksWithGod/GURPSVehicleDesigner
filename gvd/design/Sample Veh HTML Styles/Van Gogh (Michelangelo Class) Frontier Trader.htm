Van Gogh (Michelangelo-Class Frontier Trader) [TL 12]

The Michelangelo Class Frontier Trader is a well armed and defended trading vessel intended for transporting cargo in the uncertain frontier regions of the Spinward Marches and Deneb Sector. In normal operation passenger capacity is usually limited; the exact number of passengers able to be transported varies with crew compliment but three is a typical number.

The Van Gogh is the third of three Michelangelo Class ships so far constructed at the Van Trekker Yards of Pretoria. No other vessels are planned at this stage. All the Michelangelo class were ordered for a fledgling shipping line before the fifth frontier war. The Van Gogh was completed in 1107, but before the buyer could take delivery for acceptance trials the war broke out and both sister ships were lost to Vargr battle fleets. The principals in the line were killed and the firm collapsed. Title to the Van Gogh reverted to the shipyard and the ship was sealed and parked in a corner of the downport yard, awaiting a prospective buyer.

The Van Gogh was purchased late in 1112 by Captain Naiomi Taylor, late of Mertactor, in her efforts to expand her Free Trader business.
