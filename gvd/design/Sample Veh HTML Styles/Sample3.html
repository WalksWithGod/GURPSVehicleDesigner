Diamaus GMBH ICMS - 2280 Jovian class cargo vessel
View the full Jovian image
 
    The Diamaus Jovian is one of the mainstays of bulk shipping within the Imperium. Making up nearly 30% of all bulk carriers in operation within the rim sectors and nearly 9% throughout the Imperium. The design has remained virtually unchanged since the Jovian first exited the Diamaus Shipyard facility on Sol nearly 198 years ago. 
    A Jovian class bulk cargo carrier measures just under 700 feet long, 230 feet wide and 53 feet high at the bridge superstructure. 
    The ship is divided into eight sections. Below is a brief description of each section’s layout. Each deck and section is connected by one or more zero g tubes with exceptions noted below. 
Bridge Superstructure: 
    The original design of the Jovian class ship did not have a bridge superstructure. It was added when improvements in the bridge architecture and the desire to add a backup communications array and computers made it’s old location in the forward section unsuitable.  
    The bridge has crew stations for the following duties, Captain, Maneuvering, Communications, AESA, PESA, Multiscanner, Navigation and 4 Gunner positions. 
    This section contains The backup neutrino communicator, inertial navigation system, transponder, 2 microframes, a four man airlock to the ships exterior, the distress beacon system and a sandcaster with 12 missiles. The Captain’s and executive officers quarters are in this section also. 
    It is supported by it’s own full fire suppression unit and two artificial gravity units. 
Rear Center Section: 
    This section is subdivided into two sections. The section that houses the fusion reactor and the core systems of the jump drive is open with numerous catwalks and platforms for equipment access. This section that contains the medical and crew facilities is divided into four decks. It also provides access to the bridge superstructure.  
    This section contains a complete workshop, all of the medical facilities, the remainder of the crew facilities, a four man airlock to the ships lower exterior, the fusion reactor and the core systems of the jump drive. 
    It is supported by three full fire suppression units and 10 artificial gravity units. 
Port and Starboard Main Drive Sections: 
    This section is completely open with numerous catwalks and platforms for equipment access. 
    The sections port and starboard of the rear center section house the main drives, the support systems for the jump drives, two full life support systems, part of the maneuver drives and the fuel processing facilities. 
    Each section is supported by three full fire suppression units and 10 artificial gravity units. 
Main Center Section: 
    This section is a long narrow cylinder that runs down the ship’s centerline and an open framework of supports. this section is 450 ft. long 
    The top section of the cylinder is a long narrow corridor lined with cryonic capsules. This corridor open onto the second deck of the rear center section where the medical facilities are. The lower section of the cylinder contains the hydrogen tanks. 
    The cylinder is supported by 2 full fire suppression units and a artificial gravity unit. 
    Two other pressurized corridors run through the open framework providing access to the cargo bays. and turrets.  
    These corridors are each supported by a full fire suppression and by a artificial gravity unit.  
Port and Starboard Cargo Sections: 
    The sections port and starboard of the main center section are the main cargo holds and the gun turrets. 
    Each bay is supported by 4 full fire suppression units and 16 Artificial gravity units. 
Forward Section: 
    Access to this section is gained through the main center section corridors. 
    The forward section houses the main sensor and communication arrays, breaking drives, the remainder of the maneuver drives, a full life support system, docking facilities and berthing facilities for the auxiliary craft. 
    It is supported by 4 full fire suppression units and 16 Artificial gravity units.
