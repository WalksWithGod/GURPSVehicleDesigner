Missile Ordinance 
 
Solaris Arms  200mm Anti Laser System "Sandcaster"
 
    Using a similar material found in ablative personal armor and ablative foam. The sandcaster creates a dense cloud of particulates that is effective in blocking lasers, fusion guns, flamers and charged particle beams. When the cloud reaches its most efficient diameter (about 1000 yards). A small gravitic module, left in the remaining core, sends out a gravitic pulse to stop the clouds expansion. The module burns out in the process.   
    Each intervening yard of sand equals 10 HT that must be penetrated before affecting ship DR. Armor divisors apply. The cloud produced effectively blocks radar and acts as smoke. Sandcasters cannot be used in an atmosphere.   
    A contest of tactic skills resolves the effectiveness of the placement of sand in space combat. From a base effectiveness of 50% adjust the result by ±10% for each point in the winner's favor. If the to-hit roll was missed in the placement of the sand. Reduce the effectiveness of the missile by 10% for each point the roll was missed by.
